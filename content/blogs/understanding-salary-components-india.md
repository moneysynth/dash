Understanding your salary structure is crucial for financial planning. In India, your Cost to Company (CTC) consists of various components, each serving different purposes. This comprehensive guide will help you decode every element of your salary slip.

## What is CTC (Cost to Company)?

CTC is the total amount an employer spends on an employee annually. It includes direct benefits (salary components) and indirect benefits (employer contributions to PF, gratuity, etc.). However, your take-home salary is significantly less than your CTC due to various deductions.

## Key Salary Components

### 1. Basic Salary

Basic Salary is the core component of your salary and forms the foundation for calculating other components. It typically ranges from 40% to 50% of your CTC.

**Key Points**:
- Forms the base for calculating PF, Gratuity, and HRA
- Fully taxable as per your income tax slab
- Under the New Labour Code 2025, basic salary must be at least 50% of CTC
- Often includes Dearness Allowance (DA) in some organizations

### 2. House Rent Allowance (HRA)

HRA is provided to employees to cover rental expenses. It's partially or fully tax-exempt if you're paying rent.

**Tax Exemption Rules**:
- Tax exemption is the minimum of:
  1. Actual HRA received
  2. Actual rent paid minus 10% of basic salary
  3. 50% of basic salary (metro cities) / 40% (non-metro)

**Important**:
- If you own a house and don't pay rent, HRA is fully taxable
- Typically ranges from 20% to 50% of basic salary
- Keep rent receipts for tax filing

### 3. Provident Fund (PF)

Both employee and employer contribute 12% of basic salary to EPF (Employee Provident Fund). There's a minimum cap of ₹1,800 per month.

**Breakdown**:
- **Employee Contribution**: 12% of basic salary (capped at ₹1,800 minimum)
- **Employer Contribution**: 12% of basic salary
  - 8.33% goes to EPS (Employee Pension Scheme)
  - 3.67% goes to EPF

**Key Points**:
- PF is deducted from your gross salary, reducing your take-home
- Employer PF contribution is part of CTC but not part of take-home salary
- PF contributions are tax-exempt up to ₹1.5 lakh per year under Section 80C

### 4. Gratuity

Gratuity is a retirement benefit paid by employers. It's calculated as 4.81% of basic salary and is deducted from gross salary.

**Eligibility**:
- Payable after 5 years of continuous service
- Formula: (Last drawn salary × 15/26) × Number of years of service

**Tax Benefits**:
- Employer contribution is part of CTC but not take-home
- Tax-exempt up to ₹20 lakh

### 5. Special Allowances

These include various allowances like transport, medical, food, and other special allowances.

**Tax-Exempt Allowances**:
- **Transport Allowance**: Up to ₹1,600/month is tax-exempt
- **Medical Allowance**: Up to ₹15,000/year is tax-exempt (with bills)
- **Food Coupons**: Up to ₹2,600/month is tax-exempt

**Other allowances are fully taxable**

### 6. Gross Salary

Gross Salary = Basic Salary + HRA + Special Allowances + Other Benefits

However, from gross salary, employer contributions (PF, Gratuity) are deducted to arrive at the amount available for other components.

### 7. Deductions

**Common Deductions**:
- **Employee PF**: 12% of basic (min ₹1,800)
- **Professional Tax**: Varies by state (typically ₹200-₹2,500/year)
- **Income Tax (TDS)**: Based on your tax slab and investments
- **Other Deductions**: Insurance, loan EMIs, etc.

### 8. Take-Home Salary

Take-Home Salary = Gross Salary - (Employee PF + Professional Tax + Income Tax + Other Deductions)

This is the actual amount credited to your bank account every month.

## Example: Salary Breakdown

Let's say your **Annual CTC is ₹12,00,000**:

**Monthly Breakdown**:
- Monthly Gross Salary: ₹1,00,000
- Basic Salary (50%): ₹50,000
- HRA (40% of Basic): ₹20,000
- Special Allowances: ₹30,000

**Deductions**:
- Employee PF (12%): -₹6,000
- Professional Tax: -₹200
- Income Tax (approx): -₹8,000

**Take-Home Salary**: ₹85,800

## New Labour Code 2025 Impact

The New Labour Code 2025 mandates that basic salary must be at least 50% of CTC. This affects:

- Higher PF contributions (since PF is calculated on basic salary)
- Higher gratuity (calculated on basic salary)
- Potentially lower take-home salary initially
- Better retirement benefits in the long run
